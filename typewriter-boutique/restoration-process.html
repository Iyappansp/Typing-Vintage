Step-by-Step

How We
Restore

From initial inspection to final certification, every stage is documented and reversible. We restore history — we don't erase it.

Average hours 22 hrs

Step 01
Intake & Documentation

Every machine enters our workshop photographed from 12 angles. Serial numbers are cross-referenced against manufacturer records. A detailed intake form records all visible damage, missing parts, and cosmetic condition — this becomes the baseline for our restoration log.

Photography Serial Verification Condition Report
Step 02
Full Disassembly

The carriage is removed. Typebars are individually indexed and photographed before removal. Every screw, spring, and lever is catalogued in our parts tray system. We work in sections — never rushing, never forcing. Stuck fasteners are freed with penetrating oil and patience, not force.

Precision Tools Penetrant Oil Parts Indexing
Step 03
Ultrasonic Cleaning

Type slugs, linkage arms, and small metal components go into our ultrasonic bath at 37kHz. Decades of dried ink, dust, and oil are removed without abrasion. The result: parts as clean as the day they left the factory — critical for precise mechanical alignment.

Ultrasonic Bath Degreaser Compressed Air
Step 04
Mechanical Overhaul

The escapement — the heart of any typewriter — is adjusted with a watchmaker's precision. Typebar alignment is checked against a reference card at ±0.1mm tolerance. Spring tensions are measured and corrected. The platen rubber is tested with a Shore durometer and replaced if hardness exceeds acceptable limits.

Alignment Gauge Escapement Tool Durometer
Step 05
Cosmetic Restoration

We clean and preserve original finishes — never repainting unless absolutely necessary. Decals are carefully revived with conservation-grade products. Small chips may be touched up using colour-matched enamel. Chrome parts are polished to as-original specification.

Renaissance Wax Enamel Touch-Up Chrome Polish
Step 06
Certification & Delivery

A full type test is conducted — every character, every key. The complete restoration log is compiled and printed. The certificate of authenticity is signed by our workshop director. The machine is packed in a custom hardwood crate with humidity-buffering foam and delivered with white-glove handling.

Authentication Crate Packaging White Glove Delivery
