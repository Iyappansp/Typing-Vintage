The Hermes 3000 is widely regarded as one of the finest portable typewriters ever manufactured. This 1966 example was acquired from an estate in Lausanne and underwent our full restoration protocol over 22 hours. The seafoam-green casing shows minor patina consistent with its age, while all mechanical systems operate flawlessly.

Restoration Log

22 hours total · Completed February 2025

Day 1 · Intake & Assessment
Full photographic documentation. Serial number verified against Hermes factory records. Carriage removed; typebars inspected individually. 3 bent typebars identified, mechanism intact.
Day 2–3 · Disassembly & Cleaning
Complete disassembly to component level. Ultrasonic cleaning of type slugs. Platen removed; rubber measured at Shore A 42 — within acceptable range, no replacement required.
Day 4–5 · Mechanical Overhaul
Escapement adjusted and regulated. Margin stops recalibrated. 3 typebars realigned to ±0.1mm tolerance. All pivot points lubricated with clock oil.
Day 6 · Cosmetic & Final
Casing cleaned with isopropyl and waxed with Renaissance Wax. Touch-up applied to two small paint chips. Ribbon installed. Full type test — 26 character passes, all clean impressions.
Certificate Issued
Authentication certificate signed by workshop director. Serial record lodged with KeyLegacy database. Machine approved for sale.
